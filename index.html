About Sri Muktsar Sahib

Sri Muktsar Sahib is a historic city in Punjab,
revered in Sikh history for the bravery of the 40 Mukte
who laid down their lives alongside Guru Gobind Singh Ji in the Battle of Muktsar in 1705.

History of Sri Muktsar Sahib

Sri Muktsar Sahib, formerly known as Khidrana, holds immense historical and spiritual significance in Sikh history. The city became sacred after the Battle of Muktsar in 1705, where 40 Sikh warriors (Mukte) sacrificed their lives fighting alongside Guru Gobind Singh Ji against the Mughal army. The place was renamed “Muktsar,” meaning “the pool of liberation,” in honor of these martyrs.

Temples in Sri Muktsar Sahib

Shiv Mandir

This is one of the oldest temples in the city, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It attracts devotees especially during Mahashivratri.

Durga Mandir

Famous for Navratri celebrations, Durga Mandir is a spiritual place known for its peaceful ambiance and community gatherings.

Hanuman Mandir

This temple is dedicated to Lord Hanuman and is known for its Tuesday prayers and beautiful architecture.

Mela Maghi – Sri Muktsar Sahib

Mela Maghi is a major annual religious festival held in Sri Muktsar Sahib in January to commemorate the martyrdom of the 40 Mukte who fought bravely for Guru Gobind Singh Ji. Thousands of devotees visit the city during this time to take a holy dip in the Sarovar and participate in the Nagar Kirtan, religious fairs, and cultural celebrations. The Mela reflects devotion, courage, and the rich heritage of Sikhism.
